Voter Eligibility Basics

Before registering, you must meet these criteria:

  • Citizenship: You must be a citizen of India.
  • Age: You must be 18 years or older on the qualifying date.
  • Residence: You must be an ordinary resident of the constituency.
  • Sound Mind: You must not be disqualified under any law.

Election Timeline Explorer

Understand the critical phases of an election cycle. Click any phase to expand.

Phase 1

Notification

The Election Commission formally announces election dates. The Model Code of Conduct comes into effect immediately, restricting what parties and candidates can do.

Key activities: Press conference by Chief Election Commissioner, gazette notification, deployment of election observers.

Phase 2

Nomination

Candidates file their nomination papers along with affidavits declaring assets, criminal records, and educational qualifications.

Key activities: Scrutiny of nominations by Returning Officer, withdrawal period, final list of contesting candidates published.

Phase 3

Campaigning

Parties hold rallies, distribute manifestos, and campaign across constituencies. This strictly ends 48 hours before polling begins ("silence period").

Key activities: Rallies, door-to-door campaigns, social media outreach, expenditure monitoring by observers.

Phase 4 — Current Focus

Polling Day

Voters cast their ballots at designated polling booths using EVMs. VVPAT machines provide a paper trail for verification.

Key activities: Voter ID verification, indelible ink marking, EVM voting, VVPAT slip verification, sealed storage of EVMs.

Phase 5

Counting & Results

Votes are securely counted at central counting centers and official results are declared constituency by constituency.

Key activities: Round-by-round counting, VVPAT audit sampling, declaration of results, certificate of election issued.
